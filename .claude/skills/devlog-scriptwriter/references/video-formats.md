# Video Format Templates

7 proven formats for indie game devlog YouTube Shorts. Each format is a complete template: hook structure, beat-by-beat outline, visual suggestions, and duration guidance.

General rules:
- Target duration: **30-47 seconds**
- Hook must land in **first 3 seconds**
- Every format follows: **Pre-hook visual (0-1s) -> Opening line (1-3s) -> Story (3s+)**
- Visuals drive, voice follows -- voiceover comments on what viewer sees

---

## The Bug

**When to use:** Something broke in a funny, dramatic, or surprising way during development.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: The funniest moment of the bug in action (slow-mo or replay)
- Opening line template: "This bug broke my [feature] for [timeframe]." / "[Thing] was supposed to [X]. Instead it [Y]."

**Structure (4 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. The break | 0-5s | Show the bug happening -- the funnier/weirder, the better | Set up what was supposed to happen |
| 2. The struggle | 5-15s | Show attempts to fix -- code changes, test runs, more failures | Narrate the debugging process with honest frustration |
| 3. The fix | 15-25s | Show the code change or approach that worked | Explain what was actually wrong (one technical sentence + plain English) |
| 4. The result | 25-35s | Show it working correctly -- satisfying comparison | Understated relief. Forward momentum ending. |

**Visual suggestions:** Split-screen before/after. Show the actual code diff if it is small and readable. Replay the bug in slow-mo for comedy.

**Duration:** 30-40 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Video #3 "I Broke Physics" -- Troll punches crate, crate flies off level. "Okay... a bit too strong."

## The Satisfaction

**When to use:** A mechanic, visual, or sound effect looks/feels satisfying. The footage sells itself.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: The most satisfying moment, potentially in slow-mo or with a replay
- Opening line template: Minimal or none -- let the visual breathe. If voiceover: "I could watch this all day." / "[Number] hours to get this right."

**Structure (3 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. The reveal | 0-10s | Show the satisfying thing happening -- full screen, clean capture | Minimal. Maybe one line of context. Let the visual carry. |
| 2. The making-of | 10-25s | Show how it was built -- brief process, key moments | Light narration on the technical approach. Keep it short. |
| 3. The replay | 25-35s | Show it again from a different angle, or in slow-mo, or in context of gameplay | Close with a specific detail or tease. |

**Visual suggestions:** Slow motion. Multiple angles. Before (ugly) -> after (satisfying). Screen recording with clean UI.

**Duration:** 25-35 seconds (shorter is fine -- satisfaction videos are visual-first)

**Pavlo example:** Crate destruction sequence in video #3 -- the moment crates shatter perfectly with music swell.

## Before/After

**When to use:** A feature, visual, or system went through a visible transformation.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: Quick flash of the "after" state (the payoff)
- Opening line template: "This is what [feature] looked like [timeframe] ago." / "[Feature] used to look like this."

**Structure (3 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. Before | 0-10s | Show the old/broken/ugly version | Set context -- when this was, why it looked like this |
| 2. The change | 10-25s | Show the process of transformation -- key steps, not everything | Narrate what changed and why (specific decisions, tools used) |
| 3. After | 25-40s | Show the new version in full glory -- lingering shot | Understated reaction. One line about what is next. |

**Visual suggestions:** Side-by-side comparison. Split screen. Quick cut between old and new. Time-lapse of the process.

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Any visual upgrade or mechanic improvement. The ragdoll physics before (chaos) vs after (controlled throw) from video #6.

## The Decision

**When to use:** A design decision or dilemma -- choosing between two approaches, almost deleting something, making a trade-off.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: Show what was almost lost/changed/deleted
- Opening line template: "I almost deleted [feature]." / "I had to choose between [X] and [Y]." / "This feature almost didn't make it."

**Structure (4 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. Context | 0-7s | Show the feature/system in question | What exists and why a decision was needed |
| 2. The dilemma | 7-18s | Show both options -- what each looks like in practice | Present the trade-off honestly. No right answer. |
| 3. The choice | 18-30s | Show the chosen approach in action | Explain why. Reference a game design principle or another game. |
| 4. The outcome | 30-40s | Show the result of the decision | Was it worth it? What was lost? Forward momentum. |

**Visual suggestions:** Show BOTH options so the viewer can form their own opinion. Quick toggle between them. The decision point should feel real, not manufactured.

**Duration:** 35-45 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Video #6 -- "Less realistic, but much better gameplay -- a classic gamedev trade-off" (choosing to push whole body vs single bone for throw mechanic).

## The Trick

**When to use:** A clever solution, shortcut, or insight that made something work. One key idea that changed everything.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: The result of the trick in action
- Opening line template: "One line of code changed everything." / "The fix was [surprisingly simple thing]." / "[Small change] made [big difference]."

**Structure (4 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. The problem | 0-8s | Show what was not working | Set up the problem clearly -- what was wrong |
| 2. The insight | 8-18s | Show the "aha" moment -- the code, the diagram, the idea | Explain what clicked. One sentence of insight. |
| 3. The solution | 18-28s | Show the implementation -- the actual change | Walk through the fix. Keep it simple. |
| 4. The proof | 28-40s | Show it working perfectly | Let the result speak. Understated reaction. |

**Visual suggestions:** Show the actual code change if small. Diagram the insight. Before/after comparison of the behavior.

**Duration:** 30-40 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Video #6 -- "Instead of pushing one bone, let's push the whole body at once" (the insight that fixed rotational chaos).

## The Fail

**When to use:** Something took significant effort but did not work out, was scrapped, or had to be completely redone.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: The moment of failure/realization
- Opening line template: "A week of work. In the trash." / "I spent [time] on [thing]. None of it made it into the game." / "[X] hours. Zero usable results."

**Structure (4 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. The investment | 0-8s | Show the work being done -- montage of effort | Set up how much time/effort went in |
| 2. The problem | 8-18s | Show what went wrong -- the breaking point | Honest about what failed and why |
| 3. The kill | 18-28s | Show deleting/scrapping/reverting the work | The moment of letting go. Self-deprecating humor here. |
| 4. The lesson/pivot | 28-40s | Show what came next -- the new approach or the empty space | What was learned. Or just: "back to square one." |

**Visual suggestions:** Time-lapse of work. The delete/revert moment. Empty project after cleanup. Side-by-side of failed attempt vs what replaced it.

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Any scrapped feature or approach that did not work out during development.

## The Number

**When to use:** A specific number tells the story -- iterations, attempts, hours, lines of code, versions.

**Hook formula:**
- Pre-hook visual: Quick montage of iterations/attempts
- Opening line template: "[Number] iterations to get this right." / "[Number] hours. [Number] attempts. One result." / "Version [number] of [number]."

**Structure (4 beats):**

| Beat | Duration | Visual | Voiceover |
|------|----------|--------|-----------|
| 1. The scale | 0-7s | Quick cuts showing many attempts/versions | The number. Let it land. |
| 2. The process | 7-20s | Show key iterations -- early bad, middle OK, late better | Narrate the progression. What changed each time. |
| 3. The worst one | 20-28s | Show the worst attempt/funniest failure | Self-deprecating moment. "This was attempt 12." |
| 4. The final | 28-40s | Show the final result | Understated pride. Was it worth the iterations? |

**Visual suggestions:** Grid/montage of all versions. Counter overlay. Quick cuts between iterations. Side-by-side worst vs final.

**Duration:** 30-45 seconds

**Pavlo example:** Applicable to any feature that took multiple attempts -- physics tuning, animation iterations, level design passes.

## Format Selection Guide

When choosing a format for Pavlo's dev progress:

| Dev Situation | Best Format | Why |
|---------------|-------------|-----|
| Something broke hilariously | **The Bug** | Bugs are relatable + funny |
| A mechanic looks/feels great | **The Satisfaction** | Visual dopamine drives shares |
| Major visual improvement | **Before/After** | Transformation is satisfying |
| Had to choose between approaches | **The Decision** | Trade-offs are interesting |
| Found a clever solution | **The Trick** | "Aha" moments are engaging |
| Scrapped significant work | **The Fail** | Authenticity builds trust |
| Lots of iterations | **The Number** | Scale creates impression |

**Performance pattern from Pavlo's data:** Videos #3 and #6 (best performers) used The Bug and The Trick formats respectively. Problem-solution structure consistently outperforms simple showcase.
